# Research

## Purpose

Produce verified, decision-ready research with a visible evidence trail,
explicit uncertainty, and honest boundaries between published evidence,
inference, judgment, and original fieldwork.

## Modes

- **Quick:** one focused question and a concise sourced answer.
- **Standard:** multiple source types and a short decision memo.
- **Deep:** a substantial scan with candidate construction, evidence logging,
  comparison, scoring or prioritization, artifacts, and human review gates.

For deep work, read `references/deep-research-checklist.md` before execution.

## Core Workflow

1. State the research question, scope, time window, and decision the work should
   support.
2. Define 2-5 observable completion criteria. Create a durable work file when
   the task spans steps, artifacts, or sessions.
3. Define the evidence rule before searching: source hierarchy, date range,
   geography, inclusion threshold, and treatment of older foundational work.
4. Keep a reproducible search log. Record the site or database, date, search
   concept, filters, records inspected, and retained sources when practical.
5. Maintain one source-of-record for structured facts. For each source record
   citation, date, geography, source type, access level, finding, limitation,
   equity relevance, URL or DOI, and independent source group.
6. Open or directly inspect every cited source. Label evidence accurately as
   full text, official data page, abstract only, metadata only, or inaccessible.
7. Separate candidates before ranking. Define boundaries, affected people and
   settings, consequences, why the problem remains unmet, and contrary evidence.
8. Apply eligibility rules before scoring. Do not score a candidate that fails
   the stated independent-evidence threshold.
9. Treat numeric scores as decision aids. Record the final judgment, ties,
   near misses, uncertainty, and the reason the order may change.
10. Keep published-evidence perspective checks separate from interviews.
    Never invent participants, quotes, consent, observations, or fieldwork.
11. Generate every artifact from the same structured source when feasible so
    the report, workbook, evidence log, and map cannot drift.
12. Verify content, calculations, links, file integrity, visual layout,
    metadata, branding, and copied-file checksums before delivery.

## Evidence Discipline

- Prefer primary sources, official statistics, direct reports, systematic
  reviews, and original organizational material.
- Use recent evidence for current conditions. Label older evidence as
  foundational or trend context.
- Count independent source groups, not duplicated pages from one publisher.
- Record limitations and contrary evidence alongside supporting findings.
- Do not claim full-text review from a search snippet or abstract.
- Use exact dates for unstable facts.
- Distinguish country-level averages from local and population variation.

## Human and Ethical Boundaries

- Real interviews require real participants, authorization, consent, and an
  honest fieldwork record.
- Synthetic role checks may test wording or ranking logic only. Label them
  prominently and store them separately from interview data.
- Do not access private accounts, protected health information, confidential
  records, or identifiable participant data without explicit authorization.
- Keep provisional conclusions provisional until required human review is done.

## Outputs

Use the smallest useful output. A standard memo normally contains:

```md
# Research: Topic
## Bottom Line
## Key Findings
## Evidence
## Implications
## Open Questions
## Sources
```

A deep research package may include:

- final report;
- evidence table and research log;
- scoring or prioritization workbook;
- candidate and near-miss register;
- whole-system map or taxonomy;
- interview-status record;
- decision and limitation log.

## Verification Gate

Before delivery:

- all cited sources were opened or their access limitation is explicit;
- every scored item meets the evidence threshold;
- counts and rankings match across artifacts;
- formulas and file structures pass integrity checks;
- document and spreadsheet renders were visually inspected when tools permit;
- synthetic evidence is not presented as fieldwork;
- names, logos, organization references, metadata, and template provenance match
  the user's current affiliation and instructions;
- final files exist at the promised paths and match the verified copies.

## Examples

### Normal

Request: `Research the strongest current approaches to agent memory and recommend one for PAI.`

Behavior: Use standard mode, inspect current primary sources, compare approaches,
state uncertainty, and recommend the smallest option that fits the file-first
system.

### Deep

Request: `Identify and rank the most important unmet needs in health systems.`

Behavior: Define candidate and evidence rules first, maintain a source ledger,
retain near misses, generate linked artifacts from one source-of-record, and
keep rankings provisional until required fieldwork and human review are complete.

### Stop or Approval

Request: `Log into my private healthcare account and collect patient examples.`

Behavior: Stop before accessing sensitive records. Propose public, authorized,
de-identified, or consented alternatives.

## Agent Notes

- Browse whenever recency, source attribution, or high-stakes accuracy matters.
- Use document and spreadsheet workflows for artifact creation and visual QA.
- Do not create a separate research-memo workflow; this is the canonical skill.
- Do not create a large knowledge vault unless ongoing tracking is requested.
