# Deep Research Checklist

## Setup

- Research question, decision, scope, geography, dates, and exclusions stated
- Completion criteria and human review owner stated
- Output directory and durable work record established
- Existing templates checked for current branding and hidden metadata

## Evidence Model

- Search log created before or during discovery
- Structured source ledger is the source-of-record
- Access level recorded: full text, official page/data, abstract, metadata, inaccessible
- Independent source groups distinguished from duplicate publications
- Limitations, contrary evidence, and equity implications recorded
- Older sources labeled foundational or trend evidence

## Candidate and Ranking Controls

- Candidate boundaries prevent avoidable double counting
- Directly experienced needs and underlying conditions are distinguished
- Eligibility threshold applied before scoring
- Scores, final rank, ties, near misses, and rationale all retained
- Ranking marked provisional when fieldwork or human review is pending

## Fieldwork Boundary

- Actual interviews have participants, authorization, consent, and dates
- Synthetic perspective checks are labeled “not interviews”
- No invented quotes, observations, participants, or approvals

## Artifact Consistency

- Report, workbook, research log, and map derive from the same structured data
- Counts, names, scores, ranks, citations, and dates match everywhere
- Workbooks include README, evidence, scoring, search, decision, and interview-status views when appropriate
- Reports state what is complete, partial, and pending

## Delivery QA

- Links and citations checked
- Formula and archive-integrity scans pass
- DOCX and spreadsheet renders visually inspected
- No clipped text, split rows, accidental blank pages, or unreadable figures
- Logos, organization names, headers, footers, filenames, properties, and embedded media scanned
- Delivered copies match verified files by checksum
- Durable work record updated with outputs and remaining actions
